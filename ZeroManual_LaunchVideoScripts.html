Script Option 01

"Built for This"

A bold manifesto. The founder speaks first, the team amplifies, the world responds.

2:00 – 2:30 min
Punchy → Building
Black screen + VO
Low drone → cinematic swell
Direct-to-lens, close
Frequent, white/minimal
On-Screen Role

Co-Founder

Anchor of the video. Opens, closes, and carries the manifesto. Direct to camera throughout.

Team Member 1

The Research Voice

Speaks to depth of understanding — why ZeroManual studies industries differently.

Team Member 2

The Builder Voice

Speaks to what is being built — the infrastructure angle, not just software.

Team Member 3

The Vision Voice

Speaks to the bigger picture — Asia's enterprises, the stakes, the moment.

Cold Open 0:00 – 0:18

VISUAL: Pure black screen for 2 seconds. Then — fast cut montage. No music yet. Only ambient sound. A factory floor in Bangladesh. A logistics hub at 3AM. A shipping container port. A distribution centre. Workers, machines, movement. Real. Unglamorous. Alive.

Co-Founder — Voiceover (not yet on camera)

"These are not background businesses. These are the operations the global economy depends on."

Delivery note: Quiet. Certain. Not dramatic — just true. Let the images carry the weight.

Asia's industries. Running the world.

White text, center-screen. Hold 2 seconds. Fade.

Founder — On Camera 0:18 – 0:50

Co-Founder — Direct to Camera

"The AI revolution is real. But almost everything being built right now was designed for someone else — for stable regulatory environments, for clean data, for English-language systems, for markets that look nothing like Dhaka or Jakarta or Karachi.

When those systems arrive here... they break. Not because the technology is wrong. Because it was never researched for this."

Shot: Medium close-up. Slightly off-centre framing. Plain background or soft industrial setting. Holds eye contact. Not reading — speaking.

Team Member 1 0:50 – 1:05

Team Member 1 — Direct to Camera

"Most AI companies skip the research. They take a product they already built and configure it for a new client. We don't do that. Before we architect anything, we go deep — into the workflows, the exception cases, the failure modes that destroy generic systems in month three."

Shot: Similar framing to founder. Different environment — perhaps near a whiteboard, desk, or natural window light. Conversational, not rehearsed.

Team Member 2 1:05 – 1:18

Team Member 2 — Direct to Camera

"What we build is not software configured to a client's settings. It's AI infrastructure — designed to become the operational foundation of an enterprise. And to compound in value over time."

Shot: Slightly tighter. The word "infrastructure" should feel weighty — pause slightly before it.

Not software. Infrastructure.

Text appears on cut, white, minimalist. 1.5 seconds.

Team Member 3 1:18 – 1:32

Team Member 3 — Direct to Camera

"The enterprises that move now are the ones that will own their categories. The window is open. It will not stay open forever. And for the first time, someone is building AI specifically for how these businesses actually operate."

Shot: Medium shot. Slight lean-forward energy — urgency without shouting.

Founder — Return 1:32 – 2:00

VISUAL: Brief B-roll return — same factories, same ports, same logistics centres. But now the music swells. Energy shifts. These are no longer scenes of a problem. They are scenes of opportunity.

Co-Founder — Direct to Camera

"We are ZeroManual. We are an AI research and deployment company building the infrastructure that transforms what Asian enterprises are capable of — enterprise by enterprise, industry by industry.

If you are building one of those enterprises — we should talk."

Shot: Return to the same framing as opening. Slight smile on last line — earned, not performed. Confident stillness.

Zero Manual.
The AI backbone of industry.

Final card. Hold 3–4 seconds. Website URL below. Clean fade out.

Editing & Production Style — Script 01

Desaturated, slightly cool. Industrial tones. Deep shadows. Not too polished — keep it raw and credible.

Hard cuts throughout. No dissolves. Brief pauses between speakers to let the words land.

Cold open: silent or ambient noise only. Builds from 0:18 onward — low, textured, cinematic. Full swell at final B-roll sequence. No lyrics.

White. No-frills. Medium-weight sans-serif. Appears on hard cuts — never animated in fancy ways. Feels like a statement, not a design element.

Minimal. Mostly locked-off. Slight push-in on key lines. Handheld only for B-roll.

16:9 for primary. Export a 9:16 vertical cut for social reels — team member lines work well as individual clips.

Script Option 02

"We Went In"

Origin story format. The founder explains the founding insight personally — why ZeroManual exists from lived observation, not theory.

2:30 – 3:00 min
Steady, story-driven
Founder, mid-sentence
Warm, subtle, consistent
Interview-style + walkaround
Sparse — only for impact
On-Screen Role

Co-Founder

Tells the story personally. Slightly more casual framing — like they're explaining to a friend what they've seen and why it matters.

Team Member 1

The "We Studied It" Voice

Validates the research angle — what deep operational study actually looks like at ZeroManual.

Team Member 2

The "We Built It" Voice

Explains the deployment reality — what AI infrastructure means in practice.

Team Member 3

The "This Is The Moment" Voice

Speaks to the timing and urgency — why this matters now, not in five years.

Founder — Cold Open 0:00 – 0:22

Co-Founder — Slightly Casual, Mid-Thought Energy

"I kept seeing the same thing happen. A company in manufacturing, logistics, distribution — a genuinely world-class operation — would try to bring in AI. They'd spend months on it. And by month three, the system was either abandoned or so heavily modified it barely functioned.

And the reason was always the same. The AI was built for a different world."

Shot: Medium close-up. Could be walking and then stopping to address camera directly. Natural setting — an office, a workspace. Warm lighting. Feels like a conversation, not a speech.

B-Roll 0:22 – 0:35

VISUAL: Shots of real Asian enterprise environments. Manufacturing. Freight. A call centre. A distribution warehouse. Busy, complex, real. Not stock footage aesthetic — grittier if possible.

Co-Founder — Voiceover during B-Roll

"These businesses — in Dhaka, Jakarta, Karachi, Ho Chi Minh City — they're not peripheral. They are running the global economy. And they've been handed AI that doesn't know they exist."

Team Member 1 0:35 – 0:52

Team Member 1 — Conversational

"When we go into an industry, we're not going in to configure a product. We study how the industry actually works — the logic underneath the workflows, the exception cases that nobody writes down, the failure modes that only show up in live operations. That research is what we build from."

Shot: Interview framing — slight off-axis. Could be seated. Relaxed but focused. Feels like someone explaining something they genuinely care about, not a sales pitch.

Founder — Continues 0:52 – 1:15

Co-Founder — Direct to Camera

"ZeroManual is an AI research and deployment company. The research is not a preamble — it is the thing. It is the reason what we build works when everything else has failed.

We start from inside the operational reality of an enterprise and its industry. And then we build the AI infrastructure that transforms what that business is structurally capable of."

Shot: Return to original framing. Slightly more energy than the opening — building to something.

Team Member 2 1:15 – 1:28

Team Member 2

"An enterprise that runs on ZeroManual isn't just more efficient. It processes complexity that used to require proportional headcount growth. It scales without the drag. When a competitor tries to take its market — they find the gap they expected has closed."

Shot: Walking-and-talking optional here for energy shift — or a static close-up. Confident delivery.

The gap they expected no longer exists.

Text-only card here works well — pause on black for 1 second after this scene before cutting to Team 3.

Team Member 3 1:28 – 1:42

Team Member 3

"Every quarter without serious AI infrastructure is a quarter where the competitive gap compounds. The enterprises that move now will own their categories. We're at the beginning of that window — and we're building the companies that will still be standing when it closes."

Shot: Slightly tighter. Direct. This is the urgency beat — deliver it without theatrics. Let the words carry it.

Founder — Close 1:42 – 2:20

VISUAL: B-roll of the ZeroManual team working — informal, real. Not staged. Whiteboards, screens, conversations. Interspersed with final enterprise imagery. Music peaks here.

Co-Founder — Voiceover / Return to Camera

"We're building the AI backbone of Asian industry. In the markets that power the global economy. For the enterprises that are ambitious enough to see what's at stake.

That's what ZeroManual is. That's what we're here to do.

If you're building one of those enterprises — we should talk."

Shot: Open with voiceover over B-roll, then cut back to founder direct-to-camera for the final three lines. Hold on their face for 1 full second after the last word before cutting to end card.

Zero Manual.
The AI backbone of industry.

Final card — warm tone, not cold black this time. Website + CTA. Hold 4 seconds.

Editing & Production Style — Script 02

Warmer than Script 01. Still grounded and real — but slightly golden. Earthy tones. Think documentary about something important, not a product ad.

Longer takes. Fewer cuts. Let the speakers breathe. A couple of slow push-ins during key lines to build intimacy without movement.

Subtle throughout from the start — low, textured strings or ambient piano. Builds steadily. Never overpowers the voice. Peaks on final B-roll before the close.

Use sparingly — 2–3 maximum. Only for the single most important lines. When they appear, give them space. White or very light cream. Fade in, don't slam.

Slow focus racks between key moments. Handheld for team B-roll. Locked-off for dialogue. One tracking shot in the middle B-roll sequence for visual variety.

16:9. This one works beautifully as a full film. For social, clip the founder cold open (0:00–0:22) as a standalone teaser — ends on the hook.

Reference Videos

Watch these before production. Each demonstrates a specific element worth borrowing.

01
Manifesto tone · direct-to-camera · text overlays

Stripe — "Increase the GDP of the internet"

youtube.com/watch?v=4tW8Y0sHMjM

Stripe's early company story video. Founder-led, minimal production, but deeply confident. The way Patrick Collison speaks directly at you — certain without being aggressive — is the exact energy Script 01 is aiming for. Study how they use silence and pauses.

02
Origin story format · warm documentary feel

Notion — "We're building something different" (2019 Launch)

youtube.com/watch?v=gqCuCHJnoOo

Exactly the tone of Script 02. Founder speaks like a human, not a pitch deck. The B-roll of the team working is authentic and functional — not over-produced. Notice how team members add context without ever sounding like they're reading a script.

03
Problem framing · urgency · B2B enterprise energy

Palantir — "The difference between a good decision and a great one"

youtube.com/watch?v=LtFtbVguPJY

Watch this for how a B2B enterprise AI company frames its value proposition on film — serious, specific, never dumbed down. The B-roll of complex real-world operations is exactly the visual language ZeroManual should be using.

04
Asia-focused · enterprise narrative · visual tone

Grab — "Everyday Superapp" Brand Film

youtube.com/watch?v=kfzSWrtlgYk

For the B-roll visual language specifically. Grab shoots Southeast Asian operational reality — motorbikes, markets, logistics, people — in a way that feels authentic rather than exploitative. This is the visual register ZeroManual's B-roll should aim for.

05
Cold open · no-music hook · text cards

Andreessen Horowitz — "Software is eating the world" short-form video

a16z.com / search "software eating the world video"

For the cold-open approach in Script 01 specifically. Watch how silence and ambient sound in the first 10 seconds creates more tension than any music could. The text-card cadence is also worth studying — sparse, declarative, no flourishes.
